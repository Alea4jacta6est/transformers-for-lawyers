<span class="coj-bold">Legal context</span>

<p class="coj-title-grseq-3">
<span class="coj-italic">Directive 93/13</span>

<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">

<p class="coj-count" id="point3">3</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">The 13th and 24th recitals of Directive 93/13 state:</p>
<p class="coj-normal">‘Whereas the statutory or regulatory provisions of the Member States which directly or indirectly determine the terms of consumer contracts are presumed not to contain unfair terms; whereas, therefore, it does not appear to be necessary to subject the terms which reflect mandatory statutory or regulatory provisions and the principles or provisions of international conventions to which the Member States or the Community are party; whereas in that respect the wording “mandatory statutory or regulatory provisions” in Article 1(2) also covers rules which, according to the law, shall apply between the contracting parties provided that no other arrangements have been established;</p>
<p class="coj-normal">…</p>
<p class="coj-normal">Whereas the courts or administrative authorities of the Member States must have at their disposal adequate and effective means of preventing the continued application of unfair terms in consumer contracts.’</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point4">4</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">Article 1 of that directive provides:</p>
<p class="coj-normal">‘1.   The purpose of this Directive is to approximate the laws, regulations and administrative provisions of the Member States relating to unfair terms in contracts concluded between a seller or supplier and a consumer.</p>
<p class="coj-normal">2.   The contractual terms which reflect mandatory statutory or regulatory provisions and the provisions or principles of international conventions to which the Member States or the Community are party, particularly in the transport area, shall not be subject to the provisions of this Directive.’</p>
</td>
</tr>

</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">Article 3 of Directive 93/13 defines the conditions under which a term included in a contract concluded between a seller or supplier and a consumer is to be regarded as unfair.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">

<p class="coj-normal">Article 6(1) of that directive provides:</p>
<p class="coj-normal">‘Member States shall lay down that unfair terms used in a contract concluded with a consumer by a seller or supplier shall, as provided for under their national law, not be binding on the consumer and that the contract shall continue to bind the parties upon those terms if it is capable of continuing in existence without the unfair terms.’</p>

<p class="coj-normal">‘Member States shall ensure that, in the interests of consumers and of competitors, adequate and effective means exist to prevent the continued use of unfair terms in contracts concluded with consumers by sellers or suppliers.’</p>

<p class="coj-normal">‘§ 1.   Interest on a sum of money shall be payable only if it results from a legal transaction or from a law, court decision or decision of another competent authority.</p>
<p class="coj-normal">§ 2.   If the interest rate is not otherwise specified, statutory interest shall be payable at a rate corresponding to the sum of the reference rate of the National Bank of Poland plus 3.5 percentage points.</p>
<p class="coj-normal">§ 2<span class="coj-super">1</span>.   The maximum interest rate resulting from a legal transaction may not exceed twice the statutory interest rate per annum (the maximum interest).</p>
<p class="coj-normal">§ 2<span class="coj-super">2</span>.   If the interest rate resulting from a legal transaction exceeds the maximum interest rate, the maximum interest shall be payable.</p>
<p class="coj-normal">§ 2<span class="coj-super">3</span>.   Contractual terms may not exclude or limit provisions relating to the maximum interest, including where foreign law has been selected. In such a case, the provisions of this Law shall apply.</p>

Under that agreement, the consumer also undertook to pay PLN 600 (approximately EUR 139) in arrangement fees and PLN 3400 (approximately EUR 790) in administrative fees. </td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">However, Directive 2008/48 does not contain either the concept of ‘non-interest credit costs’ or provisions governing the issue of limiting credit costs or the sharing of those costs between the seller or supplier and the consumer. It is apparent from the documents before the Court that the ‘non-interest credit costs’ constitute a subcategory of the ‘total cost of the credit’ referred to in Article 3(g) of Directive 2008/48, as that cost encompasses all costs, including, in particular, interest.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point41">41</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">Point (g) of Article 5(1) of that directive, which lists the pre-contractual information that must be provided to the consumer prior to the conclusion of the agreement, and point (g) of Article 10(2) thereof, which concerns the information that must be specified in the agreement in a clear and concise manner, lay down an obligation to inform the consumer of the ‘total amount payable by the consumer’, defined in Article 3(h) of Directive 2008/48 as being ‘the sum of the total amount of the credit and the total cost of the credit to the consumer’. Those provisions do not lay down any obligation to provide information concerning either the non-interest credit costs or the method whereby those costs are to be calculated.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point42">42</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">Consequently, it must be held that Directive 2008/48 does not lay down any harmonised provisions concerning ‘non-interest credit costs’ or the way in which those costs are to be calculated.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point43">43</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">Although Directive 2008/48 harmonises only certain aspects of the Member States’ rules concerning agreements covering credit for consumers, it is clear from recital 44 thereof that, in order to ensure market transparency and stability, and pending further harmonisation, Member States should ensure that appropriate measures for the regulation or supervision of creditors are in place (see, to that effect, judgment of 6 June 2019, Schyns, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A467&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑58/18</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A467" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2019:467</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A467" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">44</a>). However, it is necessary to ensure that those measures do not conflict with the areas harmonised by Directive 2008/48.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point44">44</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">In that regard, it should be borne in mind that Directive 2008/48 was adopted in order both to ensure that all consumers in the European Union enjoy a high and equivalent level of protection of their interests and to facilitate the emergence of a well-functioning internal market in consumer credit (judgments of 5 September 2019, Pohotovosť, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A665&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑331/18</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A665" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2019:665</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A665" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">41</a>, and of 19 December 2019, Home Credit Slovakia, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A1130&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑290/19</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A1130" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2019:1130</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A1130" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">28</a> and the case-law cited).</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point45">45</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">Article 10 of that directive lists the information that must be specified in a clear and concise manner in the agreement. The Court has already held that Article 10(2) of that directive provides for full harmonisation as regards the information which must imperatively be included in a credit agreement (judgment of 5 September 2019, Pohotovosť, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A665&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑331/18</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A665" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2019:665</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A665" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">50</a>).</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point46">46</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">In addition, as has been recalled in paragraph 38 above, under Article 22(1) of Directive 2008/48, in so far as that directive contains harmonised provisions, Member States may not maintain or introduce in their national law provisions diverging from those laid down in that directive.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point47">47</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">In the present case, it is apparent from the documents before the Court that the provisions of national legislation concerning non-interest credit costs confine themselves to establishing a ceiling value for and a method for calculating those costs, together with the consequences of failing to comply with that ceiling value. In any event, as the Advocate General noted in points 59 and 60 of his Opinion, it is for the referring court to verify whether the national legislation imposes additional information obligations.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point48">48</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">In the light of the foregoing considerations, Article 3(g), Article 10(2) and Article 22(1) of Directive 2008/48 must be interpreted as not precluding a provision of national legislation which lays down a calculation method regarding the maximum amount of non-interest credit costs that may be imposed on the consumer, provided that that provision does not introduce additional information obligations regarding those non-interest credit costs which go beyond those laid down in Article 10(2) of that directive.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p class="coj-title-grseq-2">
<span class="coj-bold">
<span class="coj-italic">The second question</span>
</span>
</p>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point49">49</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">By its second question, the referring court asks, in essence, whether Article 1(2) of Directive 93/13 is to be interpreted as meaning that a contractual term which establishes non-interest credit costs in accordance with the ceiling value set by a provision of national legislation, without necessarily taking the costs actually incurred into account, falls outside the scope of that directive.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point50">50</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">In that regard, it should be borne in mind that Article 1(2) of Directive 93/13, which refers to terms which reflect mandatory statutory or regulatory provisions, introduces the possibility of exclusion from the scope of that directive, provided that, according to the case-law of the Court, two conditions are satisfied. Firstly, the contractual term must reflect a statutory or regulatory provision and, secondly, that provision must be mandatory (judgments of 7 November 2019, Kanyeba and Others, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A936&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑349/18 to C‑351/18</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A936" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2019:936</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A936" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">60</a>, and of 3 March 2020, Gómez del Moral Guasch, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2020%3A138&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑125/18</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2020%3A138" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2020:138</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2020%3A138" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">31</a>).</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point51">51</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">In order to establish whether those conditions are satisfied, the Court of Justice has held that it is for the national court to determine whether the contractual term concerned reflects mandatory provisions of national law that apply between the parties to the contract independently of their choice or provisions that are supplementary in nature and therefore apply by default, that is to say, in the absence of other arrangements established by the parties (judgment of 3 March 2020, Gómez del Moral Guasch, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2020%3A138&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑125/18</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2020%3A138" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2020:138</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2020%3A138" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">32</a> and the case-law cited).</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point52">52</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">The Court of Justice has held that a national court must take account of the fact that, having regard to the purpose of that directive, namely the protection of consumers against unfair terms included in contracts concluded with consumers by sellers or suppliers, the exception introduced by Article 1(2) thereof is to be strictly construed, in order to ensure its effectiveness (see, to that effect, judgments of 10 September 2014, Kušionová, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2014%3A2189&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑34/13</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2014%3A2189" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2014:2189</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2014%3A2189" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">77</a>, and of 20 September 2018, OTP Bank and OTP Faktoring, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2018%3A750&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑51/17</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2018%3A750" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2018:750</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2018%3A750" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">54</a> and the case-law cited).</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point53">53</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">In any event, it should be borne in mind that a contractual term included in a contract concluded by a seller or supplier with a consumer falls outside the scope of that directive only if that contractual term reflects the content of a mandatory statutory or regulatory provision for the purposes of Article 1(2) of Directive 93/13, read in the light of the 13th recital thereof (see, to that effect, judgment of 10 September 2014, Kušionová, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2014%3A2189&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑34/13</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2014%3A2189" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2014:2189</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2014%3A2189" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">80</a>).</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point54">54</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">Such an exclusion has been justified by the fact that it may legitimately be supposed that the national legislature has struck a balance between all the rights and obligations of the parties to certain contracts, a balance which the EU legislature has expressly intended to preserve (judgments of 21 March 2013, RWE Vertrieb, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2013%3A180&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑92/11</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2013%3A180" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2013:180</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2013%3A180" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">28</a>, and of 3 April 2019, Aqua Med, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A282&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑266/18</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A282" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2019:282</a>, paragraph <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A282" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">33</a>).</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point55">55</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">While the Court of Justice has, in its case-law, developed the criteria for interpreting Article 1(2) of Directive 93/13, it is for the referring court to draw specific consequences from those criteria.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point56">56</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">In the present case, it should be noted, subject to the verifications to be carried out in that regard by the referring court, that it does not appear that a contractual term which confines itself to implementing a method for calculating the ceiling value of non-interest credit costs ‘reflects’, strictly speaking, the provision of national legislation under consideration (see, to that effect, judgment of 3 April 2019, Aqua Med, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/AUTO/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A282&amp;locale=en" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xml;notice=branch">C‑266/18</a>, <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A282" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/pdf">EU:C:2019:282</a>, paragraphs <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A282" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">35</a> and <a class="coj-CourtLink" href="./../../../legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=ecli:ECLI%3AEU%3AC%3A2019%3A282" hreflang="en" onclick="target='CourtTab';" type="application/xhtml+xml">36</a>).</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point57">57</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">Indeed, that provision does not appear, in itself, to determine the rights and obligations of the parties to the contract, but confines itself to restricting their freedom to set the non-interest credit costs above a certain level and in no way prevents the national court from reviewing the possible unfairness of the way in which such costs are set, even below the statutory ceiling value.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<col width="5%"/>
<col width="95%"/>
<tr>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-count" id="point58">58</p>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">In the light of the foregoing considerations, Article 1(2) of Directive 93/13 must be interpreted as meaning that a contractual term which establishes non-interest credit costs in accordance with the ceiling value set by a provision of national legislation, without necessarily taking the costs actually incurred into account, does not fall outside the scope of that directive.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</table>

<td valign="top">
<p class="coj-normal">Since these proceedings are, for the parties to the main proceedings, a step in the action pending before the national court, the decision on costs is a matter for that court. Costs incurred in submitting observations to the Court, other than the costs of those parties, are not recoverable.</p>
